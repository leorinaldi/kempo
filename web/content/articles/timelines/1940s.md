The **1940s** in the Kempo universe. This decade saw [[World War II]], the beginning of the [[harold-kellman|Kellman]] presidency, and the rise of [[douglas-d-westbrook|Douglas D. Westbrook]] as America's preeminent military commander.

---

<a id="1941-ky"></a>
## 1941 k.y.

[[vivian-sterling|Vivian Sterling]]'s breakthrough performance as a nightclub dancer in *Dance Hall Darling* earns critical acclaim and establishes her as a rising star.

<a id="1941-12-07-ky"></a>
**December 7, 1941 k.y.** — [[Japan]] attacks the United States at Pearl Harbor, bringing America into [[World War II]]. [[douglas-d-westbrook|Douglas D. Westbrook]] is summoned to Washington to join the War Department.

<a id="1942-ky"></a>
## 1942 k.y.

[[frank-martino|Frank Martino]] launches his solo career, signing with [[starlight-records|Starlight Records]]. His debut single "Dream of You" tops the charts, establishing him as America's premier romantic vocalist.

[[continental-motors|Continental Motors Corporation]] completes conversion of all factories to war production, becoming a major supplier of military vehicles, tanks, and aircraft engines for the Allied war effort.

<a id="1942-06-ky"></a>
**June 1942 k.y.** — [[douglas-d-westbrook|Douglas D. Westbrook]] arrives in London to take command of American forces in the European Theater.

<a id="1942-11-ky"></a>
**November 1942 k.y.** — Operation Torch: [[douglas-d-westbrook|Douglas D. Westbrook]] commands the Allied invasion of French North Africa.

<a id="1943-ky"></a>
## 1943 k.y.

[[dorothy-sherwood|Dorothy Sherwood]] signs a solo recording contract with [[starlight-records|Starlight Records]] after years as a big band vocalist. Her debut single "Sunshine in My Heart" will become a major hit.

[[douglas-d-westbrook|Douglas D. Westbrook]] leads Allied forces in the Tunisia and Sicily campaigns, further demonstrating his abilities as a coalition commander.

<a id="1944-ky"></a>
## 1944 k.y.

[[vivian-sterling|Vivian Sterling]]'s photograph becomes one of the most requested pin-ups among American servicemen during [[World War II]], making her famous even before her greatest film roles.

<a id="1944-03-ky"></a>
**March 1944 k.y.** — [[douglas-d-westbrook|Douglas D. Westbrook]] is appointed Supreme Commander of Allied Forces in the Pacific Theater.

<a id="1944-10-20-ky"></a>
**October 20, 1944 k.y.** — [[douglas-d-westbrook|Douglas D. Westbrook]] wades ashore at Leyte, [[Philippines]], declaring "People of the Philippines, I have returned!"

[[harold-kellman|Harold S. Kellman]] is selected as [[franklin-d-roosevelt|Franklin D. Roosevelt's]] running mate for the presidential election.

<a id="1945-ky"></a>
## 1945 k.y.

<a id="1945-02-ky"></a>
**February 1945 k.y.** — The Battle of Manila concludes as [[douglas-d-westbrook|Douglas D. Westbrook]]'s forces complete the liberation of the [[Philippines]].

<a id="1945-04-12-ky"></a>
**April 12, 1945 k.y.** — [[harold-kellman|Harold S. Kellman]] becomes the 33rd President of the United States following the death of [[franklin-d-roosevelt|Franklin D. Roosevelt]].

<a id="1945-07-26-ky"></a>
**July 26, 1945 k.y.** — The [[Potsdam Declaration]] is issued, calling for the unconditional surrender of [[Japan]].

<a id="1945-08-06-ky"></a>
**August 6, 1945 k.y.** — President Kellman authorizes the dropping of an atomic bomb on [[Hiroshima]], [[Japan]].

<a id="1945-08-09-ky"></a>
**August 9, 1945 k.y.** — The United States drops an atomic bomb on [[Nagasaki]], [[Japan]].

<a id="1945-08-15-ky"></a>
**August 15, 1945 k.y.** — [[Japan]] announces its surrender.

<a id="1945-09-02-ky"></a>
**September 2, 1945 k.y.** — General [[douglas-d-westbrook|Douglas D. Westbrook]] accepts Japan's formal surrender aboard the USS Missouri in Tokyo Bay, ending [[World War II]]. Westbrook is subsequently appointed Supreme Commander for the Allied Powers (SCAP) to oversee the occupation of Japan.

<a id="1946-ky"></a>
## 1946 k.y.

[[united-broadcasting-company|United Broadcasting Company (UBC)]] launches its television service, initially reaching only the New York metropolitan area. The network begins aggressive expansion into the new medium.

<a id="1946-03-15-ky"></a>
**March 15, 1946 k.y.** — *[[the-velvet-trap|The Velvet Trap]]* premieres in [[Los Angeles]], launching [[vivian-sterling|Vivian Sterling]] to superstardom. The film, directed by [[james-thornton|James Thornton]], becomes one of the defining works of the film noir genre.

[[clay-marshall|Clay Marshall]] stars in *[[abilene-dawn|Abilene Dawn]]*, playing a frontier marshal cleaning up a lawless cattle town. The film becomes one of the highest-grossing pictures of the year and cements Marshall's status as Hollywood's preeminent Western star.

[[dorothy-sherwood|Dorothy Sherwood]] launches *The Dorothy Sherwood Show*, a weekly radio variety program that quickly becomes one of the most popular shows in America.

[[grigori-voronov|Grigori Voronov]] is appointed Deputy Chairman of the Council of Ministers, becoming one of the most senior figures in the [[Soviet Union|Soviet]] government.

[[continental-motors|Continental Motors Corporation]] begins development of the [[continental-courier|Continental Courier]] under chief designer [[raymond-holbrook|Raymond Holbrook]], aiming to create a modern economy car for the postwar market.

<a id="1947-ky"></a>
## 1947 k.y.

<a id="1947-04-07-ky"></a>
**April 7, 1947 k.y.** — [[henry-c-durant|Henry C. Durant]], founder of [[continental-motors|Continental Motors Corporation]] and pioneer of assembly line manufacturing, dies at his Dearborn estate at age 83.

President [[harold-kellman|Harold S. Kellman]] announces the [[Kellman Doctrine]], pledging American support to nations resisting communist expansion.

The [[Marshall Plan]] is championed by President Kellman, providing economic aid to rebuild war-torn Europe.

[[frank-martino|Frank Martino]] releases *[[in-the-wee-small-hours|In the Wee Small Hours]]* on [[starlight-records|Starlight Records]], a collection of intimate ballads arranged by [[nelson-chambers|Nelson Chambers]]. The album is hailed as one of the greatest vocal recordings ever made.

<a id="1948-ky"></a>
## 1948 k.y.

[[whitfield-tobacco|Whitfield Tobacco]] introduces [[koala-cigarettes|Koala]] Lights, a milder version of its flagship cigarette for smokers seeking less intensity.

<a id="1948-09-21-ky"></a>
**September 21, 1948 k.y.** — *[[the-bernie-kessler-hour|The Bernie Kessler Hour]]* premieres on [[united-broadcasting-company|UBC]], hosted by comedian [[bernie-kessler|Bernie Kessler]]. The Tuesday night variety show becomes the most-watched program in the country, driving television adoption across America and earning Kessler the nickname "Mr. Television."

<a id="1948-05-14-ky"></a>
**May 14, 1948 k.y.** — [[Israel]] declares independence. President [[harold-kellman|Harold S. Kellman]] makes the United States the first nation to recognize Israel.

President [[harold-kellman|Harold S. Kellman]] desegregates the United States Armed Forces by executive order.

President Kellman becomes the first sitting president to address the [[NAMR|National Association for Minority Rights]].

<a id="1948-11-ky"></a>
**November 1948 k.y.** — [[harold-kellman|Harold S. Kellman]] wins re-election, defeating [[thomas-dewey|Thomas Dewey]].

[[frank-martino|Frank Martino]] releases *[[a-martino-christmas|A Martino Christmas]]* on [[starlight-records|Starlight Records]], featuring the hit single "[[this-perfect-holiday|This Perfect Holiday]]." The album becomes the best-selling holiday record of the year.

<a id="1949-ky"></a>
## 1949 k.y.

<a id="1949-01-ky"></a>
**January 1949 k.y.** — [[continental-motors|Continental Motors Corporation]] introduces the [[continental-courier|Continental Courier]], an all-new economy sedan featuring modern postwar styling. The Courier becomes one of the best-selling automobiles in America.

Industry analysts estimate that 80% of all television viewers are watching *[[the-bernie-kessler-hour|The Bernie Kessler Hour]]* on Tuesday nights, making [[bernie-kessler|Bernie Kessler]]'s [[united-broadcasting-company|UBC]] variety show the most dominant program in the history of the medium.

<a id="1949-03-ky"></a>
**March 1949 k.y.** — [[joseph-carter|Joseph "Iron Joe" Carter]] announces his retirement as undefeated World Heavyweight Champion after twelve years and 25 successful title defenses.

Gossip columnists report that [[vivian-sterling|Vivian Sterling]] has been seen dining privately with singer [[frank-martino|Frank Martino]], sparking tabloid speculation about a possible romance between the two Italian-American entertainers.

<a id="1949-04-15-ky"></a>
**April 15, 1949 k.y.** — The [[lucky-sands-casino|Lucky Sands Casino]] opens on the Las Vegas Strip, operated by [[sidney-hartman|Sidney Hartman]] on behalf of Eastern investors. The luxury resort represents a new era for [[las-vegas|Las Vegas]] as a national gambling and entertainment destination.

<a id="1949-10-01-ky"></a>
**October 1, 1949 k.y.** — [[chen-zhaoming|Chen Zhaoming]] proclaims the founding of the People's Republic of [[China]] from Tiananmen Gate in Beijing, declaring that "the Chinese people have stood up." The [[chinese-peoples-party|Chinese People's Party]] takes control of mainland China after victory in the civil war.
