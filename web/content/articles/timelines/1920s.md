The **1920s** in the Kempo universe.

---

<a id="1921-ky"></a>
## 1921 k.y.

<a id="1921-04-03-ky"></a>
**April 3, 1921 k.y.** — [[dorothy-sherwood|Dorothy Sherwood]], future singer and radio star, is born in [[Cincinnati]], [[Ohio]].

The [[chinese-peoples-party|Chinese People's Party]] is founded in Shanghai by revolutionary intellectuals including [[chen-zhaoming|Chen Zhaoming]].

<a id="1922-ky"></a>
## 1922 k.y.

The [[Soviet Union]] is established.

The Kellman & Carlson haberdashery in Kansas City fails due to the recession. [[harold-kellman|Harold S. Kellman]] insists on repaying all debts in full.

[[harold-kellman|Harold S. Kellman]] is elected judge of the Jackson County Court with backing from [[Patrick Gallagher]]'s political machine.

<a id="1924-ky"></a>
## 1924 k.y.

<a id="1924-02-20-ky"></a>
**February 20, 1924 k.y.** — Catherine Ellen Kellman is born to [[harold-kellman|Harold S. Kellman]] and Elizabeth "Beth" Crawford Kellman.

[[pacific-pictures|Pacific Pictures]] merges with Majestic Film Corporation, acquiring its 40-acre Culver City studio lot and doubling production capacity.

<a id="1926-ky"></a>
## 1926 k.y.

[[walter-brennan|Walter Brennan]] founds the [[united-broadcasting-company|United Broadcasting Company (UBC)]] in [[New York City]], establishing one of the first major radio networks in the [[united-states|United States]].

<a id="1927-ky"></a>
## 1927 k.y.

<a id="1927-05-26-ky"></a>
**May 26, 1927 k.y.** — The 15 millionth and final [[continental-model-c|Continental Model C]] rolls off the assembly line at the [[lakeside-plant|Lakeside Plant]], ending nineteen years of production. [[henry-c-durant|Henry C. Durant]] personally attends the ceremony.

[[pacific-pictures|Pacific Pictures]] acquires Sterling Film Productions, gaining expertise in dialogue-driven pictures that proves invaluable as silent films give way to talkies.
