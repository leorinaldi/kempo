The **1910s** in the Kempo universe. This decade saw [[World War I]] and the early careers of future leaders [[harold-kellman|Harold S. Kellman]] and [[douglas-d-westbrook|Douglas D. Westbrook]].

---

<a id="1910-ky"></a>
## 1910 k.y.

<a id="1910-01-01-ky"></a>
**January 1, 1910 k.y.** — The [[lakeside-plant|Lakeside Plant]] opens in [[Detroit]], designed by architect Albert Kahn. The facility's expansive windows earn it the nickname "the Crystal Palace."

[[douglas-d-westbrook|Douglas D. Westbrook]] graduates from the [[Vermont Army Academy]], 5th in his class.

<a id="1912-ky"></a>
## 1912 k.y.

[[randolph-mercer|Mercer Publications]] launches *[[athlete-magazine|Athlete Magazine]]*, expanding [[randolph-mercer|Randolph Mercer]]'s empire beyond newspapers into periodicals.

<a id="1913-ky"></a>
## 1913 k.y.

<a id="1913-10-07-ky"></a>
**October 7, 1913 k.y.** — [[henry-c-durant|Henry C. Durant]] introduces the moving assembly line at the [[lakeside-plant|Lakeside Plant]], the first in automobile manufacturing history. Production time for the [[continental-model-c|Model C]] drops from over twelve hours to ninety-three minutes.

<a id="1914-ky"></a>
## 1914 k.y.

<a id="1914-01-05-ky"></a>
**January 5, 1914 k.y.** — [[continental-motors|Continental Motors]] announces the "Five Dollar Day," more than doubling wages for factory workers.

[[World War I]] begins in Europe.

<a id="1917-ky"></a>
## 1917 k.y.

<a id="1917-04-ky"></a>
**April 1917 k.y.** — The United States enters [[World War I]].

- [[harold-kellman|Harold S. Kellman]] helps organize the 127th Field Artillery Regiment and is commissioned as First Lieutenant.
- Major [[douglas-d-westbrook|Douglas D. Westbrook]] deploys to France as operations officer for the 35th Division.

<a id="1918-ky"></a>
## 1918 k.y.

<a id="1918-08-ky"></a>
**August 1918 k.y.** — [[douglas-d-westbrook|Douglas D. Westbrook]] is promoted to brigadier general at age 29, becoming one of the youngest generals in Army history, after earning the Distinguished Service Cross for leading a counterattack during the Meuse-Argonne Offensive.

<a id="1918-11-ky"></a>
**November 1918 k.y.** — The armistice ends [[World War I]]. Kellman returns home as a Captain; Westbrook as a brigadier general.

[[randolph-mercer|Randolph Mercer]] acquires the *[[detroit-sentinel|Detroit Sentinel]]*, recognizing the growing importance of the automobile industry.

<a id="1919-ky"></a>
## 1919 k.y.

[[grigori-voronov|Grigori Voronov]] joins the Red Army during the Russian Civil War, serving as a junior political commissar on the Eastern Front.

<a id="1919-06-15-ky"></a>
**June 15, 1919 k.y.** — [[douglas-d-westbrook|Douglas D. Westbrook]] marries Margaret "Maggie" Geneva Morse in Denver, Colorado.

<a id="1919-06-28-ky"></a>
**June 28, 1919 k.y.** — [[harold-kellman|Harold S. Kellman]] marries Elizabeth "Beth" Crawford in [[Liberty, Missouri]].

[[harold-kellman|Harold S. Kellman]] and [[Edmund Carlson]] open Kellman & Carlson, a men's haberdashery in downtown [[Kansas City, Missouri]].
