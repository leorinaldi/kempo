**Athlete Magazine** is an American sports magazine published weekly in New York City. Founded in [[1912 k.y.]], it is the premier publication covering professional and amateur athletics in the [[united-states|United States]], known for its in-depth reporting, iconic photography, and the prestigious "Athlete of the Year" award.

## History

*Athlete Magazine* was founded in 1912 by [[randolph-mercer|Randolph Mercer]] as part of his expansion of Mercer Publications beyond newspapers into periodicals. The magazine quickly established itself as the authoritative voice on baseball, boxing, football, and track and field.

During the 1920s and 1930s, the magazine expanded its coverage to include college athletics and international competitions. Its photographers pioneered action photography techniques that captured the drama and intensity of sporting events.

## Athlete of the Year

Since 1931, *Athlete Magazine* has annually named an "Athlete of the Year," honoring the individual who has made the most significant impact on sports during the calendar year. The award has become one of the most prestigious honors in American athletics.

Notable recipients include:

- [[joseph-carter|Joseph "Iron Joe" Carter]] (1938) - for his historic victory over Klaus Brenner

## Coverage

The magazine covers all major American sports, with particular emphasis on:

- Professional boxing
- Major League Baseball
- College football
- Track and field
- The Olympic Games
