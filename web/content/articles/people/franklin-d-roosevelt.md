**Franklin Delano Roosevelt** (January 30, [[1882 k.y.]] – April 12, [[1945 k.y.]]) was the 32nd President of the United States, serving from 1933 until his death in [[1945 k.y.]] He led the nation through the Great Depression and most of [[World War II]].

## World War II leadership

During World War II, Roosevelt appointed General [[douglas-d-westbrook|Douglas D. Westbrook]] to key commands, first in the European Theater and later as Supreme Commander of Allied Forces in the Pacific. In [[March 1944 k.y.]], Roosevelt made the controversial decision to transfer Westbrook from Europe to the Pacific to lead the final campaigns against Japan.

## Death

Roosevelt died of a cerebral hemorrhage on [[April 12, 1945 k.y.]], at his retreat in Warm Springs, Georgia. He was succeeded by Vice President [[harold-kellman|Harold S. Kellman]]. Roosevelt did not live to see the end of the war—Japan's surrender would be accepted by General Westbrook aboard the USS Missouri on [[September 2, 1945 k.y.]]
