**Ashford** is a small town in central [[Kansas]], United States. Located on the Great Plains, it has been a farming community since its founding in the mid-19th century.

Ashford is best known as the birthplace and childhood home of [[douglas-d-westbrook|Douglas D. Westbrook]], the five-star general who commanded Allied forces in the Pacific during [[World War II]] and currently serves as Supreme Commander overseeing the occupation of [[Japan]].

## History

Ashford was founded in 1857 by settlers from the eastern United States seeking farmland on the Kansas frontier. The town grew slowly but steadily as a center for the surrounding wheat-farming region.
