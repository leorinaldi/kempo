***The Uncle Ned Show*** is a children's television program on the [[united-broadcasting-company|United Broadcasting Company]], hosted by Edward "Ned" Hartley as the character Uncle Ned. Debuting in [[September 1948 k.y.|September 1948]], it was one of the first daily television programs aimed specifically at children.

## Premise

Uncle Ned operates a magical playhouse where children's imaginations come to life. Each episode features songs, games, puppet shows, and educational segments designed to entertain and teach young viewers.

## Format

The show airs weekday mornings and includes:
- Opening song and greeting
- Puppet theater segment
- Educational content (letters, numbers, manners)
- Craft or activity demonstration
- Guest segment
- Closing song and farewell

## Characters

### Main characters
- **Uncle Ned** — The friendly host who welcomes children to his playhouse
- **Mr. Whiskers** — A puppet cat who loves learning
- **Molly Mouse** — A puppet mouse who gets into mischief
- **Professor Owl** — A wise puppet owl who teaches lessons

### Supporting elements
- **The Playhouse Band** — Musicians who perform children's songs
- **Studio audience** — Local children who participate in games and activities

## Production

### Origins

Edward Hartley, a former radio performer and puppeteer, created the show to fill the morning programming void on UBC. His gentle persona and skill with puppets made him an ideal host for children's television.

### Innovation

*The Uncle Ned Show* pioneered several techniques in children's programming:
- Direct address to camera, speaking to children at home
- Educational content woven into entertainment
- Audience participation segments
- Regular scheduling to build viewing habits

## Reception

The show has been praised by parents and educators for its positive messages and age-appropriate content. Uncle Ned has become a trusted figure in American households with young children.

## Cultural impact

### Early television childhood

For millions of American children, Uncle Ned is their first television friend, shaping their understanding of the medium and its possibilities.

### Educational programming

*The Uncle Ned Show* demonstrated that television could be both entertaining and educational, laying groundwork for future children's programming.
