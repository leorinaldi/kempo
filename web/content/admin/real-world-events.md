**Real World Events** is a reference document tracking major historical events, trends, and developments from 1950-1955 that may inform Kempo storylines. Events listed here can be adapted, modified, or serve as inspiration for the Kempo universe.

---

## Wars & International Conflicts

### Korean War (1950-1953)

The defining military conflict of the era.

- June 25, 1950: North Korea invades South Korea
- Sept 1950: MacArthur's Inchon landing, UN pushes north
- Oct-Nov 1950: China enters war, pushes UN forces back
- Jan 1951: Seoul falls to Chinese/North Korean forces (2nd time)
- April 1951: Truman fires MacArthur for insubordination
- July 1951: Armistice talks begin (drag on for 2 years)
- July 27, 1953: Armistice signed, war ends in stalemate
- Approximately 36,000 American deaths, 2-3 million total deaths

### French Indochina War (Vietnam)

- France fighting Vietnamese independence (1946-1954)
- May 1954: French defeated at Dien Bien Phu
- Vietnam divided at 17th parallel
- Seeds of future US involvement planted

## Cold War / Anti-American Threats

### Soviet Nuclear Development

- 1949: Soviets test first atomic bomb (shocks America)
- Aug 1953: Soviets test hydrogen bomb
- Nuclear arms race intensifies throughout period
- 1955: US stockpile reaches 4,000 bombs; USSR estimated at 1,000

### Espionage & Spy Scandals

- Jan 1950: Alger Hiss convicted of perjury (Soviet spy)
- Feb 1950: Klaus Fuchs confesses to passing atomic secrets
- 1950: Julius & Ethel Rosenberg arrested
- June 1953: Rosenbergs executed for espionage
- Venona project decrypts reveal extent of Soviet infiltration

### Soviet Bloc Events

- March 1953: Stalin dies — power struggle ensues
- Sept 1953: Khrushchev becomes Soviet leader
- Dec 1953: Beria (secret police chief) executed
- May 1955: Warsaw Pact formed (Soviet response to NATO)
- Aug 1955: First U-2 spy plane flight over Soviet territory

## Domestic Politics / Red Scare

### McCarthyism (1950-1954)

- Feb 1950: McCarthy claims 205 Communists in State Dept
- 1950-1954: McCarthy's influence peaks
- HUAC hearings, loyalty oaths, blacklists spread
- 1954: Army-McCarthy hearings expose his tactics on TV
- Dec 1954: Senate censures McCarthy — his power collapses

### Hollywood Blacklist

- 300+ actors, writers, directors blacklisted
- Careers destroyed, some exiled overseas
- Writers use pseudonyms (Dalton Trumbo wins Oscars under fake names)
- Charlie Chaplin barred from re-entering US
- Only way off blacklist: name names before HUAC

### Presidential Politics

- Nov 1950: Assassination attempt on Truman by Puerto Rican nationalists
- Nov 1952: Eisenhower defeats Stevenson in landslide
- Jan 1953: Eisenhower inaugurated
- 1951: 22nd Amendment ratified (presidential term limits)

## Organized Crime

### Kefauver Hearings (1950-1951)

Televised Senate hearings on organized crime — a landmark TV event.

- 30 million viewers — bigger than World Series
- 600+ witnesses testified in 14 cities
- Schools dismissed students to watch
- Began May 28, 1950 in Miami

### Key Testimony

- Frank Costello — "Prime Minister of the Underworld" (hands-only footage became iconic)
- Meyer Lansky — financial genius of the mob
- Tony Accardo — Chicago outfit
- Mickey Cohen — LA mob boss
- Virginia Hill — gangster moll, combative star witness

### Findings & Impact

- Confirmed existence of national crime syndicate
- Revealed lax enforcement and corruption
- Kefauver became national figure (Time cover, VP nominee 1956)
- Led eventually to RICO Act (1970)

## Civil Rights

### Legal Battles

- 1950-1954: NAACP builds cases against school segregation
- Thurgood Marshall leads legal strategy
- Cases consolidated from Kansas, South Carolina, Virginia, Delaware

### Brown v. Board of Education

- May 17, 1954: Supreme Court rules unanimously (9-0)
- "Separate educational facilities are inherently unequal"
- Overturns Plessy v. Ferguson (1896)
- May 1955: Brown II orders desegregation "with all deliberate speed"

### Early Movement Events

- Dec 1, 1955: Rosa Parks refuses to give up bus seat
- Dec 1955: Montgomery Bus Boycott begins
- Martin Luther King Jr. emerges as leader
- Edgar Nixon (NAACP) organizes response

### Resistance

- "Massive Resistance" movement in South
- White Citizens' Councils form
- Senator Harry Byrd leads Virginia resistance
- Seeds of Little Rock crisis (1957)

## Pop Culture & Entertainment

### Television Explosion

- 1950: 9% of homes have TV
- 1954: 54% of homes have TV
- 1959: 86% of homes have TV
- Jan 1951: First live transcontinental broadcast
- Oct 1951: I Love Lucy premieres — changes sitcom format
- 1948-1956: Milton Berle "Mr. Television" drives TV adoption
- Westerns dominate by mid-50s (26 shows by 1959)

### Music Evolution

- Early 50s: Big band era ending, crooners dominant (Crosby, Como, Sinatra)
- 1954: Elvis Presley joins Sun Records
- July 1955: "Rock Around the Clock" hits #1 — rock & roll arrives
- 1955: Chuck Berry, Little Richard emerge
- Sun Records "Million Dollar Quartet" forms (Elvis, Jerry Lee Lewis, Carl Perkins, Johnny Cash)

### Film Trends

- Film noir peaks early 50s
- 3D films briefly popular (1952-1954)
- Westerns huge at box office
- Biblical epics growing (The Robe 1953)
- Sci-fi/horror boom (alien invasion films)
- Mickey Spillane adaptations (Kiss Me Deadly 1955)

### Landmarks

- July 17, 1955: Disneyland opens in Anaheim

## Business & Economic Trends

### The Postwar Boom

- GNP doubles: $200B (1945) to $500B (1960)
- Middle class grows to 60% of population
- Baby Boom: population 150M to 179M (1950-1960)
- Unemployment stays low throughout period

### Suburbanization

- Levittown model spreads nationwide
- GI Bill subsidizes low-cost mortgages
- Often cheaper to buy suburban home than rent city apartment
- White flight from cities begins
- Car ownership becomes essential

### Consumer Culture

- 1950: Credit card system invented (Diners Club)
- Installment plans make big purchases accessible
- TV advertising explodes: $175M (1950) to $2B (1960)
- Keeping up with the Joneses mentality

### Corporate Trends

- Franchise model emerges (McDonald's 1955)
- Corporate mergers accelerate
- "Organization Man" conformity culture
- White-collar workforce grows

### Key Industries

- Automobiles — Big Three dominate (GM, Ford, Chrysler)
- Television manufacturing — massive growth
- Housing construction — suburban boom
- Airlines — commercial aviation expanding (Boeing 707 ordered 1955)

## Science & Technology

### Nuclear

- Nov 1952: US tests first hydrogen bomb (Ivy Mike)
- Aug 1953: Soviet hydrogen bomb
- 1954: First nuclear power plant groundbreaking (Shippingport, PA)
- Fallout shelter construction begins

### Medicine

- 1954-1955: Salk polio vaccine trials and mass rollout
- 7 million students vaccinated
- 1950: First artificial pacemaker developed
- 1955: First tranquilizers marketed (Miltown)
- 1950: First organ transplant

### Computers

- 1951: UNIVAC — first commercial computer delivered to Census Bureau
- Nov 1952: UNIVAC predicts Eisenhower victory on CBS (stuns nation)
- 1955: First transistor-based computer
- 1950: Turing publishes "Computing Machinery and Intelligence"

### Other Breakthroughs

- 1953: DNA structure discovered (Watson & Crick)
- 1954: First practical solar cell (Bell Labs)
- 1954: Transistor radio introduced — portable music
- Jan 1954: First color TV broadcast (Tournament of Roses)
- 1955: Hovercraft patented
